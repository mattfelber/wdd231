The Key Tenets of Stoicism

Stoicism isn't a rigid set of rules, but rather a framework for living a virtuous and meaningful life. It's built on several core principles that offer guidance for navigating life's complexities.

Living with Virtue as Your Guide

At the heart of Stoicism lies the concept of virtue ethics. Stoics believe that cultivating virtues like wisdom, courage, justice, and temperance is essential for living a good life. These virtues act as guiding principles, helping us make sound decisions and navigate challenges with integrity.

  • Wisdom (Phronesis): The ability to use reason and good judgment to discern the right course of action in any situation.
  • Courage (Andreia): The strength to face adversity with bravery and perseverance, not in the absence of fear, but in spite of it.
  • Justice (Dikaiosynē): Acting fairly and treating others with respect, upholding what is right and just.
  • Temperance (Sophrosynē): Moderation and self-control in all aspects of life, avoiding extremes and finding a healthy balance.

By focusing on developing these virtues, Stoicism encourages us to live with purpose and integrity, contributing positively to the world around us.
